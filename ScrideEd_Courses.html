⚡ Arduino Bootcamp — Bangalore

Understand electronics. Build your first IoT device. The world's most-used learning platform for hands-on tech — in one weekend.

📅 July 2026
⏱ 2-day weekend
👤 Age 13–18
📍 Frazer Town, BLR

Arduino is the world's most widely-used platform for learning electronics and embedded systems. It's used in university engineering labs, by professional engineers, and in competitive robotics worldwide — making it one of the most valuable first skills a teenager can learn.

In this 2-day bootcamp, complete beginners go from zero to a working IoT project. Students leave with real electronics knowledge, actual code they wrote, and a physical device they built themselves.

What you'll learn
  • What Arduino is and why engineers actually use it
  • Reading inputs: buttons, sensors, temperature, light
  • Controlling outputs: LEDs, motors, buzzers, displays
  • Writing real Arduino code from scratch (not drag-and-drop)
  • Connecting components and reading circuit diagrams
  • Building and debugging a complete IoT project

Questions & Answers

Everything Parents Ask Us

Does my child need any prior experience? +
None at all. Both bootcamps are built for complete beginners. No coding knowledge, no electronics background, no prior tech exposure required. If your teen is curious, that is the only qualification.
What does a typical workshop day look like? +
Day 1 covers concepts and foundations — but hands-on from the start. Students begin assembling or coding within the first hour. Day 2 is project build day — students work on their final project and present it by end of day. No passive lectures.
What will my child actually take home? +
Their kit, their built project, and a certificate of completion. More importantly, they take home a genuine understanding of what they built and the confidence to keep going — or the clarity that this path isn't for them.
My child isn't sure if they like tech — is this still worth it? +
This is exactly who the weekend workshops are designed for. The whole point is to give teens a real, hands-on experience so they can make an informed decision. Many students who "weren't sure" discover a genuine passion. Some discover it's not for them — and that's equally valuable.
What is the batch size? +
We keep batches small — typically 8–15 students — so every student gets individual attention from the instructor. This is intentional: ScrideEd is not a mass-classroom experience.
What is the refund policy? +
Full refund if you cancel more than 48 hours before the workshop start time. 50% refund between 24–48 hours before. No refund within 24 hours, but we offer priority registration for the next available batch at no extra cost.
Can parents attend the workshop? +
Parents are welcome to drop in and observe — especially during the Day 2 project demo. We find students perform better independently, so we recommend parents stay outside the workshop room during sessions, but you're always welcome to check in.
Is the Arduino/robotics kit kept by the student? +
Yes — the kit is included in the course fee and goes home with the student. We want them to keep building after the workshop, not stop when they leave the room.
